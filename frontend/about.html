What SSE Is

SSE (Social Simulation Engine) is a deterministic simulation framework that predicts likely social outcomes by modeling how people and groups behave under constraints and incentives.

It is designed as an architecture-first prototype: the current version validates end-to-end system structure, contract integrity, and explainability.

How It Works

  1. SSM parses situation text into semantics (actors, institutions, conflict, domain, mode).
  2. ESS compiles contextual constraints and affordances.
  3. MCM contributes psychological and sociological priors.
  4. SQC scores candidate outcomes and selects the dominant one.
  5. Explainer emits a trace that ties factors to the selected outcome.

What It Does Not Do

  • It does not claim metaphysical certainty or oracle-level prediction.
  • It does not model all hidden variables in real societies.
  • It is not trained as a high-fidelity cognition model in v0.1.
  • It does not provide legal, medical, or policy guarantees.

Why This Matters

SSE focuses on transparent reasoning over opaque prediction. Every output should be inspectable via factors, alternatives, and explanation trace so users can evaluate the logic, not just the result.
